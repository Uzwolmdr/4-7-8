Introduction

Welcome to QuietMind. Protecting your privacy is important to us. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, how we protect it, and the choices you have. We wrote this policy to be clear and practical — we avoid legalese where possible while covering the important details you need to know.

Scope: This policy applies to data processed through https://quietmind.fun and any related services, pages, or subdomains operated by QuietMind, unless a different privacy notice is provided.

Information We Collect

We collect only the information necessary to provide and improve the site and services. We separate data into two categories:

  • Personal Data — Information you intentionally provide that can identify you (for example: name, email address when you subscribe, contact form entries).
  • Non-Personal / Technical Data — Data that does not by itself identify you (for example: browser type, device, IP address, pages visited, time on site, referrer, and anonymized analytics).

How We Use Information

We use collected data for the following lawful and limited purposes:

  • Service delivery: to respond to messages, deliver newsletters, or provide access to resources you request.
  • Site improvement: to analyze site usage and improve content, layout, and performance.
  • Security & fraud prevention: to detect and protect against security threats, abuse, or misuse.
  • Advertising & measurement: to display and measure the effectiveness of ads via partners like Google AdSense.
  • Legal compliance: to comply with laws, defend our rights, and cooperate with law enforcement if required.

Lawful basis

If you are in the European Economic Area (EEA), our legal bases for processing personal data typically include:

  • Consent — where you have given clear consent (for example subscribing to a newsletter).
  • Contractual necessity — to perform a service you requested.
  • Legitimate interests — to improve site operations and maintain security (balanced with users’ rights).

Cookies & Tracking Technologies

Cookies are small text files stored on your device. We use cookies for essential site functionality, analytics, and advertising. You can manage cookies via your browser settings, but disabling certain cookies may affect how the site functions.

Types of cookies we use

  • Essential cookies: Required for the core functionality (session management).
  • Preference cookies: Save language or display preferences.
  • Analytics cookies: Collect information about site use (non-identifying).
  • Advertising cookies: Used to serve and measure ads; may track activity across sites.
Manage cookies: Most browsers let you block or delete cookies. Instructions:
  • Chrome — Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox — Preferences → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari — Preferences → Privacy

Advertisements & Analytics

We use Google AdSense to display ads and Google Analytics (via gtag) to measure site usage. These third-party services collect information about your device and browsing activity. Their use of data is subject to their privacy policies.

Opt-out options

  • To opt out of Google Analytics: use the Google Analytics opt-out browser add-on.
  • To opt out of personalized ads: visit Google Ads Settings.

Measurement & aggregated reporting

We analyze aggregate data (for example: total pageviews, device distribution) to understand trends and improve user experience. Aggregate metrics do not include personally identifying details unless you have provided them intentionally (e.g. via a form).

Third-Party Services

We may share information with trusted third parties who perform services on our behalf (e.g., email delivery, analytics, hosting, ad networks). We only share the minimal data necessary for these partners to operate, and we use contractual safeguards where appropriate.

Service partners (examples)

  • Google — Analytics, Tag Manager, Ads. Their data practices are governed by Google's privacy policy.
  • Email provider — If you opt into newsletters, we use a secure email service to deliver messages and manage subscriptions.
  • Hosting provider — stores site files and handles server logs.

We do not sell your personal information to third parties.

Your Rights

You have rights regarding the personal data we hold about you. These rights vary by jurisdiction but commonly include:

  • Right to access — request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to rectification — correct inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Right to erasure — request deletion of your personal data (subject to legal exceptions).
  • Right to restrict processing — ask us to limit processing for certain purposes.
  • Right to data portability — receive a machine-readable copy of your data.
  • Right to object — object to processing based on our legitimate interests or for direct marketing.

How to exercise your rights

To exercise any of the above rights, please contact us using the details in the Contact section. We will respond to verified requests within a reasonable timeframe and in accordance with applicable law.

Security & Data Retention

We take reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect personal data from accidental loss, misuse, unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, and destruction.

Examples of our security measures

  • Encrypted connections (HTTPS) across the site.
  • Access controls and least-privilege policies for systems that store or process data.
  • Regular backups and monitoring, and security patching.

Additional Disclosures & Regulatory Notes

For European residents (GDPR)

If you are in the European Economic Area (EEA), you may have additional rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). That includes rights of access, portability, rectification, deletion, restriction, and objection. To exercise these rights, contact us at the address above.

For California residents (CCPA/CPRA)

If you are a California resident, you may have rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA). These rights may include the right to know, delete, and opt out of sale of personal information. We do not “sell” personal information as that term is commonly understood, but we do work with third-party ad providers who may use cookies to target ads.

Practical Examples

Below are a few common scenarios and how we handle them:
