Physiology: the autonomic balance

Breath is a two-way dial between the sympathetic ("fight/flight") and parasympathetic ("rest/digest") nervous systems. Short, shallow breathing tends to correlate with increased arousal, while longer, slower exhalations nudge the vagus nerve â€” and the parasympathetic system â€” into action. This physiological influence is why deliberate breath patterns can lower heart rate, reduce cortisol, and create a felt sense of safety.

When you focus your attention on the breath, you anchor the mind to immediate sensory experience. Attention training reduces rumination by providing a stable object â€” the inhale and the exhale â€” that is always present. Over time this builds attentional control and emotional flexibility.
