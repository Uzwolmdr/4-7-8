Frequently Asked Questions About Mindfulness and Anxiety

Will mindfulness make my anxiety worse?
In rare cases, practicing mindfulness can bring up difficult emotions. This is why gradual practice, guidance, and choosing appropriate supports is important. If you notice increased distress, pause the practice and seek professional support.
How long before I notice benefits?
Many people notice small improvements in mood and focus within a few weeks of consistent practice. More substantial shifts often require months of regular practice.
Can mindfulness replace medication?
Mindfulness can be a powerful complement to therapy and medication but should not be considered a direct replacement without consultation with a qualified clinician.

Short Guided Practices (Text Versions You Can Read)

3‑Minute Anchor: Sit comfortably. Close your eyes if it feels safe. Take three deep breaths, then breathe naturally. Count the incoming breath 'one', the outgoing 'two' up to three, then back to one. If your mind wanders, gently return to counting.

5‑Minute Body Scan: Lie or sit comfortably. Bring attention to your feet. Notice sensations, temperature, pressure. Move slowly upward through ankles, calves, knees, thighs, pelvis, belly, chest, hands, arms, shoulders, neck, face, and head. At each area, breathe into tension and allow it to soften.
